# Synopsis Guidelines — Bespoke Genre (Universal)

This is the **genre-neutral** synopsis guideline. It captures storytelling principles that apply across genres (sci-fi, romance, biography, slice-of-life, comedy, business, true-crime, fantasy, etc.). For genre-specific tuning, the **per-episode `_meta_supplement.md`** authored by W1-5 (meta-prompt synthesis from the user's reference scripts) layers on top of this.

---

## 1. Framework

The default synopsis framework is **20 chapters**, but the structure adapts to the target script length:

| Target script | Chapter count | Words per chapter |
|---|---|---|
| 1,500 words / ~10 min | 20 chapters | ~75 words |
| 2,500 words / ~17 min | 20 chapters | ~125 words |
| 5,000 words / ~33 min | 25 chapters | ~200 words |
| 10,000 words+ (long-form) | 30+ chapters | ~330+ words |

The chapter count flexes; the **act structure** (below) is fixed.

---

## 2. Four-act structure (universal)

| Act | Chapters (of 20) | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook + Act I — Setup** | 1–5 | Cold open, world establishment, protagonist introduction, stakes planting |
| **Act II — Rising** | 6–12 | Investigation / development / complication. Multiple possibilities, decoys, escalation. |
| **Act III — Crisis** | 13–17 | False resolution, midpoint reversal, true reveal lands at chapter ~16, climax at ~17 |
| **Act IV — Resolution** | 18–20 | Aftermath, character arc closure, thematic coda, optional outro CTA |

The **earliest-certainty lock**: the audience MUST NOT be certain of the central truth/twist before chapter 16. Chapters 1–15 hold them in active suspicion across at least two competing explanations.

---

## 3. Oscillating tension curve (mandatory)

The synopsis emotional curve oscillates — it does NOT rise monotonically:

| Pattern | Description |
|---|---|
| **Wave** | Tension rises and falls in pulses; audience gets brief relief before the next escalation. |
| **Maximum at ch.16** | The reveal beat is the global peak. Earlier peaks should be local, not global. |
| **Resonant low close** | Final chapter (20) closes on a held quiet, NOT another peak. |
| **Anti-pattern (avoid)** | Monotonic rise (boring), early peak (anticlimactic), flat middle (audience drops). |

Each chapter beat receives a tension label (Low / Medium-low / Medium / Medium-high / High / Maximum) so the curve is auditable.

---

## 4. Foreshadowing tracker (mandatory)

Every synopsis MUST include a **foreshadowing table** with at least 5 seeds:

| # | Seed | Setup chapter | Payoff chapter | Type | Status |
|---|------|---------------|----------------|------|--------|
| 1 | (e.g. an object, a phrase, a character detail) | (chapter where planted) | (chapter where it lands) | visual / verbal / character / documentary | placed → paid-off |

Rules:
- Each seed has BOTH setup and payoff. Seeds without payoff are forbidden.
- Seeds should distribute across the script (not all clustered in one act).
- The strongest seeds pay off in chapter 16+ (the reveal beat).
- Aim for at least one **callback**: a setup detail in Act I that reappears as a closing image in Act IV.

---

## 5. Decoy / suspect frames (Acts I–II)

Through chapter 15, the audience must hold at least **two competing explanations** alive. Frame them deliberately:

- The "obvious" explanation (often the eventual truth, but kept in doubt by other frames)
- The "default" explanation (what authority figures / community accept)
- An alternative (red herring / second possibility)

The reveal at ch.16 collapses the alternatives into one. Pre-ch.16 the synopsis should make the reader genuinely unsure.

---

## 6. POV / narrator structure

Decide upfront:
- **Single POV** — clean, but can feel narrow for complex topics
- **Braided POV (2–3)** — common for investigation/mystery; assign each POV a register (e.g. emotional + procedural)
- **Omniscient narrator** — direct address allowed but sparing ("dear listener" 1–2 times max)

The synopsis MUST declare the POV structure at the top and stick to it.

---

## 7. Deliverable structure

Every synopsis MUST have these sections in order:

1. **Title + meta** (genre, target length, POV, tone)
2. **Logline** (1–3 sentences)
3. **Earliest-certainty lock** (which chapter the truth lands at, why earlier is impossible)
4. **Decoy suspects / competing frames** (Acts I–II texture)
5. **Foreshadowing tracker** (table)
6. **Suspense techniques by section** (cited from `suspense_techniques.md`)
7. **Cinematic points** (visual highlights)
8. **20-chapter framework** (the actual chapter beats)
9. **Emotional curve** (oscillation check table)
10. **Discretionary inclusions disposition** (what's in, what's out, why — informed by length budget)
11. **Hand-off note for W3**

---

## 8. The per-episode supplement (`_meta_supplement.md`)

For Bespoke genre, W1-5 generates a per-episode supplement based on the user's 3–5 reference scripts and the W1 research. The supplement contains:

- **Tone / register notes** specific to this episode's audience and topic
- **Vocabulary list** for period / setting accuracy
- **Cross-reference success factors** (what worked in the user's reference scripts)
- **Avoid list** (anachronisms, clichés to skip, audience pet-peeves)
- **Benchmark callouts** (specific moves from references the writer should adapt, with attribution)

The supplement is read alongside this universal guideline. When the supplement contradicts this base (e.g. supplement says "monotonic rise works for this audience"), the supplement wins for that episode — but the writer notes it explicitly in the synopsis hand-off.
