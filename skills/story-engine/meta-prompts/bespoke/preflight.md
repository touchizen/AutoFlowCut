# Preflight Checklist — Bespoke Genre (Universal)

This preflight runs at W2 immediately after the synopsis is drafted. The synopsis MUST pass this checklist before W3 (writing) begins. If any item fails, the synopsis is revised and the preflight re-runs (max 5 rounds, escalate on round 5).

The checklist is **universal** — applies regardless of genre. The per-episode `_meta_supplement.md` may add genre/topic-specific items on top.

---

## A. Structural integrity (5 items)

1. **20 chapters present** (or the agreed length-adjusted count from `synopsis_guidelines.md` §1).
2. **Four acts identifiable** with chapter ranges matching the framework (1–5 / 6–12 / 13–17 / 18–20).
3. **Earliest-certainty lock at ch.16+** — the reveal does NOT land before ch.15.
4. **Cold open present** — chapter 1 hooks within its first 60 seconds of script equivalent.
5. **Resolution / coda present** — chapter 20 lands a final image, NOT another peak.

## B. Tension curve (3 items)

6. **Curve oscillates** — at least 3 transitions from rising to falling tension before ch.16.
7. **Maximum lands at ch.16** (or ch.16–17 for split climax). Earlier peaks are local.
8. **Final chapter closes low / resonant** — not another high.

## C. Foreshadowing (3 items)

9. **Foreshadowing tracker present** with ≥5 seeds, each with setup + payoff chapter.
10. **At least one Act-I → Act-IV callback** seed.
11. **No orphan seeds** — every setup has a documented payoff.

## D. POV / voice (2 items)

12. **POV structure declared** in the synopsis header (single / braided / omniscient).
13. **POV is consistent** — no chapter accidentally shifts POV without the structure allowing it.

## E. Decoy frames (Acts I–II) (2 items)

14. **At least 2 competing explanations** alive through ch.15.
15. **Reveal at ch.16 collapses alternatives** — the audience can name the truth at that moment.

## F. Discretionary inclusions (1 item)

16. **Discretionary disposition documented** — for every "optional" element from W1 research (specific anecdotes, side characters, period details), the synopsis explicitly says IN or OUT and why.

## G. Hand-off (2 items)

17. **Open questions for W3 listed** — the writer (W3 subagent) gets clear notes on tone register, voice anchors, vocabulary, structural compressions allowed.
18. **Word budget per chapter calculated** — total target ÷ chapter count = words/chapter, with realistic compression notes.

---

## Output format (required)

The preflight output (`05_preflight.md`) MUST be a table:

```markdown
# Preflight — ep{N} {title}

| # | Item | Pass/Fail | Notes |
|---|------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | 20 chapters present | PASS | 20 chapters confirmed |
| 2 | Four acts identifiable | PASS | 1–5 / 6–12 / 13–17 / 18–20 |
... (all 18 items)

**Round result**: PASS (0 fails) | REVISE ({N} fails — see notes)
```

If REVISE: list the failed items, the synopsis fix needed, and re-draft. Re-run preflight.

After 5 rounds without PASS: escalate to user with the residual fail list.

---

## Universal vs supplement

- This preflight is **universal** — every Bespoke-genre episode passes through it.
- The per-episode `_meta_supplement.md` MAY add additional items (e.g. "ensure period accuracy on item X", "voice register stays in country-Methodist plain") tagged as supplement-derived.
- Supplement items extend the checklist; they do not replace any universal item.
