# Preflight Check — Dark History Scripts

## Before Writing: Mandatory Verification

Run this checklist before proceeding to R5 (Script Writing).

---

## 1. Structural Integrity

### 20-Chapter Completeness
- [ ] All 20 chapters have defined beats
- [ ] Hook (cold open) is planned (written last, but planned now)
- [ ] Each act has a clear purpose and arc

### Act Balance
| Act | Target Length | Chapters | Check |
|-----|-------------|----------|-------|
| Act I | ~25% | 1-5 | Setup complete? |
| Act II | ~35% | 6-12 | Complications escalate? |
| Act III | ~25% | 13-17 | Climax satisfying? |
| Act IV | ~15% | 18-20 | Resolution earned? |

---

## 2. Mystery/Suspense Verification

### Suspect Check
- [ ] At least 2 viable suspects maintained through Act II
- [ ] Each suspect has means, motive, AND opportunity
- [ ] Red herrings are logically constructed
- [ ] Truth is NOT revealed before Chapter 15

### Foreshadowing Audit
- [ ] Every Act III revelation has 2+ seeds in Acts I-II
- [ ] Seeds are subtle enough to miss on first viewing
- [ ] Seeds are fair enough to notice on rewatch
- [ ] No "deus ex machina" resolutions

### Twist Quality
- [ ] False resolution in Chapter 15 is believable
- [ ] True revelation in Chapter 16 recontextualizes earlier events
- [ ] Twist is surprising BUT inevitable in hindsight

---

## 3. Historical Accuracy

### Fact Check Items
- [ ] Dates and timelines verified
- [ ] Historical figures portrayed accurately
- [ ] Period-appropriate language, technology, customs
- [ ] No anachronisms in setting descriptions
- [ ] Sources cited for key historical claims

### Sensitivity Review
- [ ] No glorification of violence or oppression
- [ ] Victims treated with dignity
- [ ] Cultural context provided for historical norms
- [ ] Modern perspective acknowledged where appropriate

---

## 4. Character Verification

### Protagonist
- [ ] Clear motivation established
- [ ] Has a flaw or vulnerability
- [ ] Arc shows change from beginning to end
- [ ] Viewer can empathize (not necessarily agree)

### Antagonist
- [ ] Motivation is understandable (not pure evil)
- [ ] Poses genuine threat
- [ ] Has their own logic/worldview
- [ ] Identity properly concealed (if mystery)

### Supporting Cast
- [ ] Each character serves a narrative purpose
- [ ] No redundant characters
- [ ] Relationships create tension or support

---

## 5. Forbidden Items

### Never Do
- Reveal the truth before Chapter 15 (Act III midpoint)
- Use narrator to directly explain character feelings
- Break historical facts for dramatic convenience
- Create plot holes for suspense
- Use modern slang in historical dialogue
- Resolve conflict through coincidence

### Avoid
- Monologuing villains explaining their plan
- Perfect protagonists with no flaws
- Damsels in distress without agency
- Stereotypical portrayals of historical cultures
- Gratuitous violence without narrative purpose

---

## 6. Narration Style Check

### Voice-Over Narration
- [ ] Narrator voice is consistent throughout
- [ ] Narrator provides context, not character thoughts
- [ ] Narration enhances atmosphere, doesn't replace action
- [ ] Pacing varies: fast for action, slow for tension

### Dialogue
- [ ] Period-appropriate vocabulary
- [ ] Each character has a distinct speech pattern
- [ ] Subtext present (characters don't say exactly what they mean)
- [ ] Exposition delivered naturally, not as info-dumps

---
